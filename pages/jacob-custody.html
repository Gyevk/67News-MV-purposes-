Jacob Alfonzo Taken Into Custody on Corruption Charges — Arrested in His Third Year of Office

Authorities moved swiftly Thursday morning, detaining the sitting official in his third year of service — ten years after his father first introduced him to the public as the Alfonzo family's political heir.

ML
Margaret Lowe
Senior Political Correspondent
Thursday, April 23, 2026  ·  5 min read
"The arrest was not politically motivated. The evidence speaks for itself, and no one is above the law — regardless of their last name." — Office of the Ombudsman, Thursday statement

Ten years after Governor Richard Alfonzo introduced Jacob to the world as his political heir, the man who went on to win public office is now in custody. Authorities took Jacob Alfonzo into detention Thursday morning on preliminary corruption charges, sending shockwaves through the province he has been serving for three years.

Investigators from the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) arrived at Jacob's government office at approximately 7:40 a.m., executing a search warrant and detaining him for questioning. Sources within the bureau confirmed that the detention followed months of surveillance and a review of financial records tied to several accounts linked to Jacob's office.

The timing is a bitter irony for the Alfonzo family. It was exactly a decade ago — April 2016 — that Governor Richard Alfonzo stood before cameras and tearfully introduced Jacob as the family's future. Jacob went on to enter politics, win his seat, and serve three years in office. Now, in the middle of that term, he is in custody.

What Investigators Found

According to a preliminary statement from the Office of the Ombudsman, investigators flagged a series of irregular financial transactions totaling an estimated ₱128 million across multiple accounts. The funds are believed to be connected to public infrastructure contracts awarded during Jacob's current term — contracts that, despite being under his direct jurisdiction, were allegedly redirected for personal gain.

"We have reason to believe that Mr. Alfonzo was directing public funds toward private interests in exchange for personal financial benefit," the statement read. "The scale and pattern of these transactions suggest this was not incidental."

Three Years in Office

Jacob Alfonzo won his seat in the 2023 elections, riding heavily on the Alfonzo family name and his father's political machinery. He campaigned on infrastructure development and anti-poverty programs — the same platforms his father and grandfather had used before him. He won by a comfortable margin.

In his three years in office, Jacob had maintained a relatively low public profile, rarely granting interviews and delegating most public-facing duties to his staff. Political observers now say that reticence may have been deliberate. "He was never comfortable in the spotlight," said one provincial official who asked not to be named. "We assumed it was just his personality. Now we're wondering if it was something else."

The Governor's Response

Governor Richard Alfonzo, now in his final months in office, issued a brief statement Thursday afternoon calling the detention "premature" and expressing full confidence in Jacob's innocence. The governor did not appear publicly and cancelled all scheduled engagements for the day.

"He introduced his son as the future of this province ten years ago," said one senior legislative aide who asked not to be named. "That son served three years in office. And now he's in custody. It's an extraordinary collapse."

Key Facts

₱128M in flagged transactions
3rd year of Jacob's term in office
10 years since his public introduction

What Happens Next

Jacob was held for questioning for several hours before being released on a conditional order prohibiting him from leaving the province. Formal charges are expected to be filed within the week pending the completion of the COA financial audit. His office has been placed under administrative review.

Legal analysts say the case, if it proceeds to trial at the Sandiganbayan, could become one of the most high-profile corruption prosecutions in the province's recent history — not just because of Jacob, but because of what it may reveal about the broader Alfonzo political operation spanning three decades.

For now, the governor's office remains silent. And the son who was meant to be the family's future faces a very different kind of reckoning than anyone anticipated.
