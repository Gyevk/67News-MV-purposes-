Guilty on All Counts: Jacob Alfonzo Convicted of Embezzlement, Bribery, and Buying Votes

The Sandiganbayan delivered a sweeping verdict Friday, finding the sitting official guilty across every charge filed against him — a stunning fall for a man his father introduced to the public as a political heir a decade ago.

RC
Raymond Cruz
Courts & Justice Correspondent
Friday, April 25, 2026  ·  7 min read
"The evidence presented was overwhelming, consistent, and damning. This court finds the defendant guilty on all counts." — Presiding Justice Elena Vasquez, Sandiganbayan, Friday ruling

In a verdict that stunned even seasoned court observers, Jacob Alfonzo was found guilty Friday on all charges — embezzlement of public funds, bribery of public officials, illegal vote purchasing, abuse of influence, and obstruction of a government audit. He was in his third year of office when arrested.

The trial, which lasted eleven days, laid bare a scheme that prosecutors described as "brazen, calculated, and deeply corrosive to public trust." Jacob Alfonzo, who had won his seat in the 2023 elections, was shown to have used his official position to redirect public contracts, intimidate subordinates, and purchase political loyalty — all while presenting himself publicly as a reformer continuing his family's legacy of service.

The Charges in Detail

Embezzlement: Prosecutors presented evidence that Jacob siphoned approximately ₱261 million from public infrastructure contracts over an 18-month period, routing funds through a network of shell companies registered under associates' names.

Bribery: Three sitting provincial officials testified that they received direct payments from Jacob in exchange for approving contracts and suppressing internal COA audits. One official, who turned state's witness, provided bank records and encrypted messages confirming the transactions.

Vote Buying: Perhaps the most explosive charge — evidence showed that Jacob coordinated a scheme to pay community organizers in lower-income barangays to deliver votes during the 2023 elections. Cash payments ranging from ₱500 to ₱5,000 per voter were documented across fourteen precincts.

Additional Charges: The court also found him guilty of obstruction of a COA audit and unlawful interference with a public procurement process.

Key Facts

₱261M embezzled from public funds
5 counts guilty
14 precincts affected by vote buying

Political Implications

The verdict immediately reignited calls for an investigation into Governor Alfonzo himself. Opposition lawmakers filed a formal request Friday afternoon demanding the provincial legislature open an inquiry into whether the governor had knowledge of — or involvement in — his son's activities during his term.

"You cannot tell me a man who ran this province for three terms had no idea what was happening under his own political roof," said Provincial Board Minority Leader Dana Cruz at a press conference following the verdict. "We need answers, and we need them now."

Governor Alfonzo's office released a two-sentence statement expressing that he was "deeply saddened" by the verdict and that he "respects the court's process." He did not address the calls for a legislative inquiry.

Sentencing

Sentencing is scheduled for May 14, 2026. Jacob faces a maximum combined sentence of 34 years. Legal analysts expect the court to impose a significant custodial term given the scale and deliberateness of the offenses, though his legal team has already signaled plans to appeal.

For the people of this province, the verdict closes one chapter — but opens many more questions about who else knew, who else benefited, and how deep the rot truly goes.
